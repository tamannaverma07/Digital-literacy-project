# UPI/ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUDS

A prevalent cybercrime is UPI/Online Payment Fraud, in which con artists deceive victims into sending money via digital payment apps. Usually, phony calls, messages, or QR codes are used in this fraud.
For instance, someone posing as a bank official called Rahul, a college student. The caller asked him to "verify" his UPI account, claiming there was a problem with it. Rahul was then instructed to enter his information by clicking on a link. He was then instructed to accept a "verification request" on his UPI app. Instead, Rahul approved a payment request without realizing it, and ₹10,000 was taken out of his account.
Usually, this crime is committed in three stages: first, the con artist contacts the victim and builds trust; second, they instill fear or a sense of urgency; and third, they deceive the victim into divulging private information or granting a payment request.

Typically, the target audience includes students, the elderly, and people who are not familiar with digital payments. Financial loss, stress, and a decline in confidence in online systems are among the repercussions.

This instance emphasizes how crucial it is to exercise caution when utilizing digital payment systems and to never share OTPs or authorize unidentified payment requests.
